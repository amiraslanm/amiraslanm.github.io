A Defensible CFD Validation Checklist

How to move beyond a merely converged model toward trustworthy, reportable results.

Amir Aslan Mosaferi · 2026

A defensible CFD model is not defined by attractive contours. Confidence comes from a chain of checks: correct physical formulation, suitable mesh, stable numerical solution, mesh and time independence, and comparison with reference data.

1. Define the physics before the software

Flow regime, properties, boundary conditions, turbulence model and multiphysics assumptions must be defined before building the numerical model. Many CFD errors start with an unsuitable mathematical model, not solver settings.

2. Measure mesh independence using the target quantity

At least three mesh levels are a useful starting point. Compare the decision-driving output—pressure drop, force coefficient, vibration amplitude, Nusselt number or heat-transfer rate—not residuals alone.

3. Assess convergence in multiple ways

  • Adequate residual reduction
  • Stability of monitored quantities
  • Closure of mass and energy balances
  • Physically reasonable flow-field behaviour

4. Separate verification from validation

Verification asks whether the equations were solved correctly; validation asks whether the selected equations represent reality adequately. Both are necessary and neither replaces the other.

Deliverable

A professional CFD report should document geometry, mesh, models, solver settings, convergence checks, sensitivity studies, validation and model limitations.
